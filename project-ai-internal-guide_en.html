AI Knowledge Base Site

From siloed AI know-how to an 'organizational asset' anyone can use. A project on leading the planning and creation of an interactive learning site.

Background & Challenges

Situation: Siloed AI Know-How

While AI tools had been introduced, their use was limited to a few knowledgeable members. Know-how was shared only in private chats, leading to an inefficient state where the same questions were repeatedly asked to the responsible team. A structured knowledge base for mastering AI across the organization was non-existent.

Difficulty: The "Unread Document" Barrier

Initially, we created a comprehensive text-based manual. However, it was hardly used. We learned the hard way that a document "always available" is a document "never read." We faced the fundamental challenge of how to provide engaging content that would make busy employees want to learn on their own initiative.

Our Problem-Solving Process

Needs Analysis & Content Planning

First, we thoroughly investigated how employees wanted to use AI in their work and what their pain points were, using surveys and interviews. Based on the results, we planned an interactive guide-style site where users could learn specific use cases "by purpose" and "by tool," rather than just reading feature descriptions.

Content Creation with AI Collaboration

Using the collected use cases, we leveraged generative AI to rapidly generate content drafts. Members with expertise on each tool then reviewed and supplemented the content, establishing a workflow that ensured both quality and quantity.

UI/UX Design & Interactive Features

Aiming for an "enjoyable learning experience," we designed the UI/UX. In consultation with AI, we generated HTML/CSS/JavaScript code to implement features like filters to narrow down cases by purpose or tool, and accordions that reveal details on click. We shared prototypes with the team and iterated based on feedback.

Deployment & Feedback Loop

The completed knowledge site was published on the organization's portal. We established a system to collect regular user feedback while analyzing usage data. We began operating it as a "living knowledge base," where new use cases are added as they emerge.

Value Delivered

Daily AI Integration

Established

AI became integrated into daily operations, not just a special tool.

Regular AI Usage

High Level

A significant portion of employees regularly utilized AI, establishing it as a habitual tool for efficiency.

Fostered a Learning Culture

A culture of "let's check the site first" took hold, creating an atmosphere where employees proactively seek to improve their skills.

Centralized Knowledge

Scattered know-how was systematically consolidated and elevated into an "organizational asset" accessible to everyone.

Takeaways & Replicability

From "Static Documents" to "Dynamic Experiences"

The initial struggle with the "unread document" barrier was a major learning experience. We realized that information has no value just by existing; it must be designed as an "experience" that users can relate to and explore with enjoyment. This project became a catalyst for rethinking not just how we build a site, but the very nature of knowledge sharing within the organization.

Replication Points

  • User-Centric Design: Deeply understand user needs first to plan content.

  • AI Collaboration: Use AI for content drafting and have experts review to streamline production.

  • Continuous Improvement: Establish a system for collecting feedback and updating content after launch.
