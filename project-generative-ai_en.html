Background & Challenges

Situation: Complex Communication

The SCM department manually gathered information on inventory, orders, and production schedules from multiple, disparate systems to report to stakeholders. This process took many hours daily and was prone to communication losses like missed checks and miscommunications. This resulted in delayed delivery responses to customers and slow reactions to sudden specification changes.

Difficulty: Departmental Apathy & Limited Resources

While this was a serious issue for the SCM department, other departments saw it as "business as usual," and there was little momentum for improvement. Furthermore, as a team member without a deep IT background, we had no budget for large-scale system development. The challenge was to achieve maximum impact within the constraints of existing tools and limited authority.

Problem-Solving Process

Analysis & Technology Selection

First, we thoroughly visualized the entire information flow from order to shipment—who was communicating what information, from which system, when, and to whom. By untangling this complex web, we identified the key bottlenecks. We decided to use a scripting platform as the automation "hub" because it could integrate across systems and be developed quickly.

API Integration

We investigated the API specifications of each business system and developed scripts to automatically fetch the necessary data. This built the foundation for a program to periodically and accurately collect information that staff used to copy and paste manually, breaking down information silos and paving the way for centralized data handling.

Developing Intelligent Notification Logic

We focused not just on collecting information, but also on automating its "communication."
We developed logic to post progress updates to dedicated, auto-generated threads on the chat tool for each order, mentioning only the relevant stakeholders.
This prevented information overload and missed updates.

Implementation & Measurement

We implemented the developed tool into actual operations and trained the staff. After implementation, we measured the time spent on information sharing and quantitatively proved that a task that took "many hours" was reduced to a "short time." This success was highly evaluated and sparked interest from other departments to adopt a similar system.

Value Delivered

Drastic Reduction in Work Time

Significant Shortening

Time-consuming daily manual work was significantly shortened, allowing staff to shift to higher-value core duties.

Elimination of Human Error

Eliminating data entry and transfer mistakes dramatically improved data accuracy and operational reliability.

Real-time Information Sharing

Stakeholders could now get the latest status just by checking the chat tool, drastically reducing meetings and check-in messages.

Improved Organizational Productivity

Even as workload increased, efficiency gains allowed for flexible staffing, contributing to a resilient and sustainable organizational structure.

Takeaways & Replicability

The Importance of the "User's Perspective"

Initially, we were captivated by the technical aspects and built a prototype without sufficient input from the end-users. This resulted in rework, but the experience taught me how crucial it is to "stand in the user's shoes." I believe this lesson was key to the ultimate success of a tool that the team was happy to use.

Skills Used

  • API Integration (REST)
  • Messaging API
  • Business Process Analysis & Design

Replication Points

  • Start Small: Instead of large-scale development, use existing tools (like office suites) to quickly prove value.

  • Focus on the Bottleneck: Target the most inefficient part of the workflow, such as "information gathering and communication."

  • Design the "Notification": Don't just collect data; design "who, when, and what" gets communicated to automate communication.
