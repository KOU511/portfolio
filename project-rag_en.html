Background & Challenges

Situation: Inefficient Knowledge Search Process

Internal policies were scattered across document files on a file server, making it difficult to find specific information. Keyword searches often failed to yield intended results, forcing reliance on the knowledge of specific individuals. This created a business bottleneck and raised concerns about compliance risks.

Difficulty: Limitations of Standard AI Tool Features

Initially, we simply uploaded the existing policy files into an AI note tool, but observed source referencing issues and response delays. The system couldn't answer complex questions spanning multiple policies, revealing at the prototype stage that improvements were needed for practical use.

Performance Improvement Process

Step 1: Initial Testing & Issue Identification

We uploaded the existing policy document files directly into the AI tool to measure baseline performance. We identified issues such as slow responses, inaccurate source referencing, and an inability to answer complex questions.

Step 2: Source Optimization [The Key to Success]

To maximize the AI's response performance, we focused on the structure of the source documents themselves. We converted the existing document files into a format that is easier for the AI to interpret. Furthermore, we structured the documents hierarchically and added relevant data and tags as metadata at the beginning of each file. This "data preprocessing" was the key to improving performance.

Step 3: Performance Evaluation & Measurement

We conducted before-and-after comparison tests using the same set of questions. The results showed a significant improvement in response speed and answer accuracy. Issues with source referencing were also resolved, confirming practical-level performance.

Step 4: Deployment & Feedback

We deployed the completed AI Bot to the target department. We received positive feedback, such as "search time has been dramatically reduced" and "I could find information I couldn't before," contributing to improved knowledge sharing.

Takeaways & Replicability

RAG Performance Depends on "Source Quality"

The biggest takeaway from this project is that the performance of a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system depends heavily not just on the LLM itself, but on the "quality of the source" it references. The "Garbage In, Garbage Out" principle applies to AI as well, and preprocessing and structuring information to be easily understood by the AI was the key to maximizing performance.

Replication Points

  • Structure the Source: Convert long documents into formats like Q&A to aid the AI's contextual understanding.

  • Start with a Small PoC: Use RAG-enabled tools to quickly conduct a Proof of Concept and iterate on hypotheses.

  • Evaluate Quantitatively: Use the same set of questions before and after improvements to compare and evaluate response speed and accuracy numerically.
