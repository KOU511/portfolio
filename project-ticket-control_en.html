Background & Challenges

Situation: Customer Support Siloed by Department

Inquiries from the website were first received by the responsible department, who would then review and manually assign them to relevant team members. This process was time-consuming, and because information was shared verbally or through individual chats, there was a constant risk of missed or delayed responses.

Difficulty: Lack of Clarity on "Who Has the Ball"

The biggest issue was the lack of real-time visibility into the status of an inquiryâ€”specifically, "who is handling it now, and what are they doing?" This led to inefficiencies such as delayed customer replies and multiple team members attempting to handle the same request.

Our Approach to Resolution

Automated Reception and Assignment

Inquiries from the web form are fed into an inquiry management system, which then routes them to the appropriate team based on the content, speeding up the initial response.

Real-time Database Integration

All tickets are automatically synced to a management database every 10 minutes via an automation script. This allows managers to get a real-time overview of all tickets without logging into the system.

Intelligent Notification System

Updates to the management database trigger the automation script to run. It automatically sends notifications for new and updated tickets to a dedicated channel in our chat tool as threaded messages. Team members can access the ticket directly from the chat notification.

Enhanced Features for High-Load Teams

For high-volume departments, we developed additional features including daily summary emails and a BI dashboard for a bird's-eye view. This prevents information overload and supports data-driven decision-making.

Takeaways & Replicability

Connecting Existing Tools Creates the Greatest Value

I learned that it's not always about large-scale system implementations. By connecting the various SaaS tools already in use with APIs and scripts, we can build powerful, low-cost, and rapid solutions that are perfectly tailored to the business needs.

Small "Inconveniences" Hold the Seeds of Transformation

The small "inconveniences" and "manual tasks" that team members experience daily hold the clues to dramatically improving organizational productivity. I reaffirmed the importance of identifying the root of a problem and solving it with the most appropriate technology.

Skills Used

  • Automation Scripting (e.g., GAS)
  • API Integration (REST)
  • Messaging API
  • Business Process Analysis & Design

Replication Points

  • Start Small: Instead of a large-scale development, leverage existing tools (like office suites) to quickly demonstrate value.

  • Focus on the Bottleneck: Target the most inefficient part of the workflow, often "information gathering and communication."

  • Design the "Notification": Go beyond just collecting data. Design "who, when, and what" to communicate, and automate it.
